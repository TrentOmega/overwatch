7. AI Workflows & Tactics

  • Date: 2026-03-29 Link: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DvNKYftvPW0 Summary: The actionable workflow is not a consumer prompt trick; it is a product strategy. Take a strong base model, add proprietary last-mile interaction data, then post-train with curated reward/eval data for a narrow task domain. Why it matters: This is a practical path for product teams to reach frontier-like task performance without building a frontier base model.

What it does: Produces task-specialized systems that outperform generic models in defined workflows such as support or coding assistance.

Tools/models used: Open-weight or commercial base model, proprietary interaction logs, supervised fine-tuning pipeline, RL or preference optimization, internal eval set.

Setup: 1. Pick a narrow workflow with high interaction volume and measurable quality. 2. Collect production traces, edits, outcomes, and human quality signals. 3. Build a task-specific eval set from real failures and high-value edge cases. 4. Fine-tune the base model on successful interaction data. 5. Apply preference optimization or RL on curated quality data. 6. Re-run against the internal eval set and iterate on failure clusters. 7. Deploy behind product instrumentation so new usage becomes new training data.

Available now: Yes, in principle. The gating factor is proprietary data quality, not model access.

How to use it: 1. Identify a workflow where users repeatedly correct or rank model outputs. 2. Capture those interactions as training data. 3.
